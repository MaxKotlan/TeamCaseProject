Lyndhurst Community Presbyterian Church

Learning Opportunities

Learn Creatively


We claim that education at LCPC is the faithful formation for the practice of life. Thoughtful spiritual formation allows us to claim a love that has the power to shape and transform us. All of us, from pre-schooler to faith leaders, long time members to confirmands, we all share in the discovery of what God is doing.

Children and Youth

Front Door: Ages 3 to 2nd grade - meet in Bloomfield Hall

The stories of the Bible capture the imagination and shape the beginning of faith. At LCPC we believe God is very much a part of who we are and is well at home in our heart. In this class children are invited to throw the door to their heart wide open and allow God to settle in.

Apes: 3rd - 6th grade - meet in main building, lower level

Apes is an inventive name the kids created,an acronym meaning All People Enter Story. However, do not let the name fool you. This class openly enters into scripture to find personal meaning in the unfolding story of how the generations have experienced God.

Formation Discovery: 7th grade and up - meet in the Montage Room, main building 3rd floor.

Students have learned the story of faith, formation discovery ask the next question - How do we live the story? The class draws on the wisdom of Christian spiritual practices and makes use of relevant social media technologies to help students grow into their story through application, practice, imagination and sharing.

Adults

Sunday Formation: all - meet Fellowship Room

Compelling study, discussion, books and commentary that allows individuals to examine more of who we are in Christ. This fall engage in the reading "Chasing Francis, A Pilgrims Tale" by Ian Cron. Critics write, " Reading this book may cause a total overhaul of the way you think about what it means to be a follower of Christ."

East Side Ministry: special needs adults - meets East Side Ministry Room, main building lower level

A unique classroom experience for adults with learning disabilities. Participants share in story, crafts and love followed by participation in joyful morning worship.
