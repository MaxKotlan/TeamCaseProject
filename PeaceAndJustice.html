Peace And Justice

Go Boldly and Live Gently, Our Earthly Home depends on it.


We at LCPC understand that without social justice and equality, the peace that Jesus spoke much of will be far off and that the poor and disadvantaged will continue to suffer in an unequal world.

Our Peace and Justice group meets every 4 - 6 weeks, usually on a Tuesday late afternoon or evening in the Sun Room. Come be a part of the group that led the classes / came up with the ideas for several of our successful community ministries and initiatives:

  • Saturday Tutoring Program (now in it's 7th year. Please see it's page!
  • Food 'backpacks" at local schools (~$5000 in food in the last year)
  • Community garden project grows organic produce for North Church (now ready for year 4!
  • Green Initative Team (striving for LCPC energy efficiency and climate change awareness)
Look for a film series and Pope Francis Encyclical Discussion in Early 2016 - Please join us!

We take our responsibility to the planet, and each other seriously. The care we take now helps sustains those less fortunate as well as our future generations. At LCPC we recognize the importance of sustaining God's great gift to us. We have faithfully engaged in numerous actions to live as a "green" church through our Green Initatives:

  • work to improve the energy efficiency of our buildings
  • Advent 2015 Palestinian Fair Trade Items (see John Mc)
  • Green T-shirts raise awareness and funds for energy consumption reduction
  • Support inner-city partner North Church on energy savings
  • Replaced inefficient appliances with EnergyStar rated ones
  • Performed energy audits and acted upon results
  • Host community wide green fair
  • Replaced incandescent lighting with CFL's & LEDs
  • Film screenings and held adult classes
  • Eliminated styrofoam and implemented recycling many years back
  • Shared in word and song the the February 2015 IPL Preach-In on climate change

      • Steve Ritchey or John McCumber are available to share details about our 'Green Initative' work.
